Protection as Misframing: Child Agency, Surveillance Capitalism, and India's Verifiable Parental Consent Mechanism

Sanyam Mahajan · 2026

Argues that India’s DPDP Rules construct “child protection” through surveillance architecture rather than rights frameworks — examining how Rule 10’s procedural exclusion, Rule 12’s surveillance asymmetry, and the undefined “reliable details” standard produce household profiling and LGBTQIA+ identity erasure. Framework draws on Zuboff, Fraser (misframing), Barassi, Risse, and the Puttaswamy right to privacy under Article 21.
